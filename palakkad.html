Palakkad


Palakkad District, in the southeastern part of the former Malabar district, is the largest district in Kerala. The district is nicknamed "the granary of Kerala". Palakkad is the gateway to Kerala due to the presence of the Palakkad Gap, in the Western Ghats.

How to Reach


Palakkad town consists of two railway stations, namely Palakkad Town railway station and Palakkad Junction Railway station. Palakkad has a medium-grade network of roads. The Salem-Kanyakumari National Highway 544 and the Kozhikode - Palakkad National Highway 966 run through the district.
